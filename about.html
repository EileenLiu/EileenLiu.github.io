About Me

Hello, I'm Eileen Liu. I'm a programmer artist.


I like to make beautiful code and beautiful images. To me art and programming are two mediums through which you can create a product to execute an idea, which is my passion. I enjoy building worlds, designing solutions, telling stories and solving problems.

I grew up in northern Virginia and attended Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology. There I took my first computer science classes as a freshman and fell in love with programming. I got to work on lots of cool projects, from a character creation game to software for the first satellite launched by highschool students.

I continued following my passion for building things with code in my undergraduate at Cornell University, where I'm three years into my Computer Science major and two minors in Information Science and Game Design. At Cornell I've had a variety of experiences. Taking on the role of Teaching Assistant for Introduction to Computer Game Architecture and starting an art club let me inspire others to create. Being Design and Publications Director for Women in Computing at Cornell gave me the chance to inspire women to pursue computer science. This semester at Cornell I'm currently looking forward to my courses in Graphics, Analytical Game Design, and Artificial Intelligence.

You will find that I excel at collaboration, while having many ideas of my own. I am also a fast learner and can pick up new tools and languages quickly. My background in art and programming give me a unique perspective in understanding the needs of users who aren't programmers, communication with project team members in other roles, as well as the importance of balancing aesthetics/entertainment with functionality.
